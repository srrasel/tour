Accommodation in Srinagar

The summer capital, Srinagar is unarguably one of the most visited places in Kashmir, and thus, there’s no dearth of hotels & resorts in the city. Srinagar welcomes tourists of all kind and make sure it has accommodations that cater to their different needs. The experience of staying in a houseboat over the scenic Dal and Nigeen lakes, is recommended to all kinds of travellers, especially honeymoon couples and family vacationers. Other than this, Srinagar’s staying options range from budget hotels to luxury resorts, some of which even face the beautiful Dal Lake. Even the houseboats can be divided into budget, mid-range, and luxury categories, and while one can find more budget and mid-range houseboats on Dal Lake, Nigeen Lake boasts a large number of luxury houseboats for an incredible experience. At TMI, we have association with the best hotels and resorts in order to ensure you get the finest accommodation options in Srinagar. We also offer the best deals and discounts so that you can make your hotel bookings with us at the best prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Since every season in Kashmir has its own allure, the ideal time to visit Jammu and Kashmir depends on your preferences.

Since Jammu and Kashmir is a part of India, no unique permit is needed to travel there. India just requires a visa from foreigners.

To properly take in its amazing splendour, it is advisable to set aside at least 5 to 7 days. You can select your own Kashmir travel package or even have one made specifically for your needs.

Snow usually covers the ground between February and November.

The Jammu Airport is about 8 kilometres away from the city.

The most famous exports from Jammu and Kashmir include traditional jewellery, carpets, Pashmina shawls, spices, tea, dry fruits, and embroidered garments.

Gulmarg is the honeymoon location of choice for the majority of couples.

Gulmarg is a popular destination for local and international travellers because it offers a wide range of exhilarating skiing choices in the Kashmir valley.

There are many well-known tourist destinations in Kashmir. The most well-known ones are Betaab Valley, Pahalgam, and Srinagar.Visits to Aru Valley, Gulmarg, and Sonamarg are all worthwhile.

There are various places to shop in Kashmir. The Floating Market at Dal Lake, the only one of its sort in India, should unquestionably be taken into consideration in addition to Lal Chowk, Residency Road, and other locations.

In Kashmir, there are many different kinds of plants. The four most well-known national parks in the valley are Dachigam National Park, Salim Ali National Park, Kazinag National Park, and Kishtwar National Park. Currently, the Ladakh Union Territory is home to the Hemis National Park.
